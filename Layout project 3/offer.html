PLACEMENT CELL AND CAREER GUIDANCE CELL

  • The Career Guidance Cell at The New College functions under the direct control and directions of the principal of the College. A team of Professors by they Principal, work together to guide the students on Career opportunities.
  • The objective of the cell is to identify the talents available among the students of this College and nurture and harness them further and offer guidance to choose a bright and right career.
  • The activities of the cell include collecting the database of the students and compiling them for use when needed. The cell facilitates the students to improve their skills to face various comprtitive exams such as civil service exams conducted by UPSC and TNPSC. The career Guidance Cell organaizes seminars and meetings to encourage the students to plan their future. Above all, the main function of the cell is to invite reputed companies to the campus to conduct campus interviews and find suitable jobs for the skills available among the students.
  • The cell also facilities the economically weaker sections of the students of the first and second year undergraduate courses to find suitable part time jobs to supplement the family income without affecting the academic pursuit.

SCHOLARSHIP

  • Scholorship The following scholorships are available for the students. Students are directed to meet the class counsellor/ scholoship clerk in the office for more details on various scholorships. They are also advised to regularly read the notices regarding scholorships displayed on the Notice Boards.
  • National merit-cum-means scholorships to minority communities A minimum of 50% marks in the qualifiying examination is required for applicants.(As the number of scholorships is limited, the selecgtion of eligible candidates may be done on the basis of the marks scored by the applicants in their qualifiying examination). This scholorship is available for UG, PG, M.Phil and Ph.D. courses Annual income limit: Rs. 1,00,000/-
  • National Loan Scholorship for UG and PG students beloning to SC and ST. and those converted from Hinduism to Christianity: They should be natives of Tamilnadu or domiciled therein. Annual Income limit: Rs.2,00,000/- for Sc., ST., and Rs. 1,00,000/- for Christian converts.
  • Scholarship for the physically challenged (i.e., students who are deaf / hearing impaired, visually and, Orthopedically challenged). Annual Income Limit: Rs. 1,00,000/-
  • Pondicherry state Post-Metric scholarship for students who had their education in the Union Territory of Pondicherry.
  • Residential and non-residential state scholarship for students who had their school education in the Union Territory of Pondicherry.
  • Directorate of Tribal Affairs, Goverment of Manipur, gives Post-Metric scholarships for students from Manipur State.
  • Administration of the Goverment of Union Territory of Lakshadweep, Kochi
  • Directorate of Higher Education, Kohima. Nagaland, gives Post-Metric scholarships for the students from Nagaland state.
  • Students belonging to the family of farmers community are eligble for scholarships under Tamilnadu Agricultural Laboures - Farmers welfare Scheme, offered by the Directorate of Collegiate Education, Tamilnadu.
  • Scholarships for Physically challenged Students who are natives of Tamilnadu and whose parents income does not exceed Rs. 1,00,000/-
  • The Directorate of Collegiate Education, Tamilnadu gives scholarship (stipend) to full Ph.D., scholars from all disciplines.
  • Ministry of Human Resource Development gives Scholorship to students with extrodinary academic achievements in the qualifiying examinations (80% and above), through the directorate of collegiate Education, Tamilnadu.
  • Indian Council of Cultural Relation (ICCR), Goverment of India gives scholarships to UG and PG students from Srilanka.
  • Ministry of Labour, Goverment of india, gives scholarships to the children of Beedi / Mine / Cine workers from Tamil Nadu.
  • Secondary Grade Teachers Children Scholarship is Given to the children of Secondary Grade Teachers working in Goverment Recognized Primary and Middle Schools for pursing M.A., M.Sc., M.Com., M.Ed., ML, M.C.A., M.Phil courses selection is made basis.
  • Scholarships for the first-generation students from backward classes.Annual income limit: Rs. 1,00,000/- A Certificate from the Tahsildar to the effect that there is no graduate in the family has to be produced
  • Muslim Educational Association of Souuthern India (MEASI), the parent of The New College, awards scholarships to poor and deserving Muslim students pursing unaided courses in the Day College and all UG PG course in the Evening College.
